On Language as Projection

Language often feels like a linear tool for communication, but meaning itself is rarely linear.

What we say is only a projection — a compressed version of something much larger inside.

Between what is felt and what is spoken, there is always a loss, and sometimes a transformation.

Different languages do not simply translate meaning; they reshape how meaning is structured.


语言看起来是一种线性的表达工具， 但意义本身往往并不是线性的。

我们说出来的，只是内在复杂结构的一种投影与压缩。

在“感受到”和“说出来”之间， 总是存在损失，也可能存在重构。

不同语言并不仅仅是在翻译意义， 而是在重新塑造意义的结构方式。
